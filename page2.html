Books:

What I've been reading:

Below are some of my most recent reads

  • Notes from Underground(1864)

    My introduction to Dostoevsky. This is a quick read and from what I've heard a good introduction to his work. I found it to be a captivating dive into the pychology of a bitter, isolated man and his attempts to connect with others.

  • Crime and Punishment(1866)

    After finishing Notes from Underground I delved into one of Dostoevsky's most notable works. Like its predecessor, this novel offers a profound pychological exploration of its characters, delivering a thought provoking and haunting story. While it can be slow and droning in parts, the novel is still engaging, featuring some memorable characters.

  • Nudge(2008)

    A required read for my Behavioral Economics class, this book explores how individuals and governments can use small behavioral interventions, or "nudges", to promote better decision-making and improve outcomes. I got a lot of enjoyment out of this. It was easy to follow and featured countless fascinating examples of "nudges" in practice.
